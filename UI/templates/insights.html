Insights about the decision making

The basis of this algorithm is a Million Musical Tweets, a survey that collected what songs users mentioned to
listen to on microblogs and social media. For each sample the geo location and a date is provided.

Here's an overview from where data possibly could be extracted from.


Since API requests are limited, the original application only considered data from Europe.
As the variance of meterological and cultural differences is rather high, this can be considered
as a meaningful subset.

When the weather information is gathered for the corresponding listener statistics, a dataset is composed

Having a wider range per feature shows a large variety of song types. Which is good for a learning
approach, as not only similar but much more different songs (i.e. more unique compared to each other )
are used. This indicates a good information gain and thus the database condition is sufficient for learning.

The song features to the relevant songs, come with following distribution


Which looks like a good basis for interpretation in general, as the values are well spread. The closer the
samples are located within a feature to each other, the less difficult it is to train a regressor (as there are
less cases to differentiate) but also most likely less reliable information can be derived from resulting estimates.
